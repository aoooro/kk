03 · The Brief

Maximise The Moment

Make Project X the biggest, most fun thing Krystal Klear has ever put out, and give fans the song they will want playing all summer.

The measure of success is a clear, undeniable step-up in KK's performance as an artist. We hold it lightly, with no chart targets and no obligation to tie it to the album campaign.

Success looks like

  • Biggest first-week of any KK release to date
  • Monthly listeners pushed above the current ~115K trajectory
  • Real creation volume on TikTok and Reels, from a near-standing start
  • Ubiquity in DJ sets

04 · The Track

Don't Overthink It

Project X is an unapologetic summer banger. It is maximalist, a little absurd, and pure fun – and that is exactly the point.

Krystal Klear has spent fifteen years earning his credibility. This is the sound of him cutting loose and enjoying it.

A World Between Brat And The Best Indie Disco

Read into it as much or as little as you like. If you want the deeper version: Project X lives in the space between Charli xcx's BRAT era and the golden run of indie disco, roughly 2004–2012. DFA-flavoured, New York-coded, bombastic. A credible underground producer making a record built for maximum joy.

07 · The Strategy

Hand The Fans Their Song Of The Summer

We give fans an irresistible summer anthem and make it effortless for them to make it their own – by dancing to it, filming it, requesting it, and singing the chorus back.

Lean In ✅
  • Fun, maximalist, confident energy
  • The hook, repeated everywhere
  • The day-to-night transition (hotel, beach, club)
  • Club-first credibility, letting the DJs build it
  • Phone-first, spontaneous, high-volume content
  • Handing fans the easiest ways to take part
Avoid ❌
  • Overthinking or over-polishing it
  • "KK goes pop" framing, or anything that reads as a cynical pivot
  • Positioning it as the album's prologue
  • Being too precious
  • Volume for the sake of volume, with no hook behind it

11 · Creative Territories

The World Of Project X

Territory 01

The Hook On Loop

The chorus as a hypnotic, repeatable motif across visualiser, cutdowns, captions and metadata.

Territory 02

Day To Night

The lyric's own arc. Beach and pool by day, club and dancefloor by night. Sun, water, sweat. Our core visual story, and the spine of the content formats.

The name is a gift. Chaotic summer-party energy, the house party that spills into a rave. Branded and serialised.

Visual Language

Maximalist and saturated. It borrows the boldness and energy of BRAT without emulating it or trying to extend that era, and sits it next to the grain of 2004–2012 indie disco.

16 · Moments

The Fun Stuff

Celebrity Seed → Micro-Creator Wave

Famous friends post with the sound for a credible kickoff. We follow fast with a ~£1k micro-creator wave so it feels like a trend that caught fire. Surgical around the chorus.

"Soundtrack Your Summer"

The creator format: adopting a familiar transition format: hotel (GRWM) → beach → club. "the assignment is clear. no notes".

Project X-tra, The Party

A small day-to-night party (pool & beach by day, rave by night) tied to summer live dates as a recurring branded series.

⚑ Flag · The Creator Approach (Diverse vs Bikini-Led)

We recommend a broader, all-genders day-to-night approach as the core. It reaches more of the actual summer audience rather than narrowing the song to one demographic; it is safer and more flexible for brand and platform; it reflects Dec's values and his stated discomfort with the "boys-club" side of the industry; and it ages better. The bikini-led angle stays available as an optional layer – it clearly would resonate to an audience on TikTok.

17 · Moments

DJ Keep Playing My Song

We seed people into the crowd at Krystal Klear shows, holding their phones up and pointed at KK as he plays. Every screen scrolls the same line – DJ KEEP PLAYING MY SONG.

It turns what is usually a meme – annoying clubbers trying to get the DJ's attention to play a song – into a playful idea. The clubber doesn't even need to be pointing it at KK; they could be dancing anywhere, arm aloft, holding their phone with the message and Project X playing as the embedded sound.
